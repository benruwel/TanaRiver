Roads and Public Works

Tana River County has a total of 1,108 Km of classified road network. Out of this, about 55 percent of the total road network is in good condition.
Most of the roads are intercepted by seasonal rivers (commonly known as laghas , which make them impassable during the rainy seasons. Although the county produces most of the marketed mango fruits in major towns in the Coast region, most of the produce ends up rotting in farms due to poor road network. The county government therefore, has to allocate adequate resources for scaling up road improvement and opening up new roads.
